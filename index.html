About me

I graduated from Franklin and Marshall College in 2014 with a B.A. in Astrophysics. While at F&M I worked with Dr. Fronefield Crawford on pulsar identification algorithms and an extragalactic pulsar survey.

I recieved my Ph.D. from the Department of Astronomy at Cornell University working with Dr. Lisa Kaltenegger on modeling the climates of potentially habitable exoplanets. While at Cornell I also worked with Dr. Natasha Holmes and Dr. Andrea Stevenson Won on using virtual reality in physics education.

In the Fall of 2020 I will begin my Master of Fine Arts degree at the Rhode Island School of Design. In their 2-year Digital + Media program I will focus on exploring our relationship with science by using it as a creative tool.

Research

From modeling life on other worlds to virtual reality in the classroom.

My recent work focuses on the role of surface color on the habitability of exoplanets. Using 1D climate and photochemistry models we can explore how planets behave under different conditions and construct references for future observations of potentially habitable exoplanets.

Art

Research inspired

We may find evidence for life on another planet in the next decade but we will never get a photo of what these worlds look like in our lifetime. My art attempts to realize these exoplanets using our most current understanding of the science.
